30Enmebaragesi9006First Dynasty of Kish030Who made the land of Elam submit4First archaeologically attested king on the list, believed to have reigned sometime in the 28th or 27th century BCE. Name translates to "Priest who permates the throne", or "Crown fit for a ruler". Four insciptions have been found bearing his name, one of which mentions him as king. Believed to have ruled over all of northern Babylonia and part of southern Babylonia. Believed to have led a successful military campaign against Elam, marking the earliest historical mention of the land of Elam and one of the earliest conflicts for which contemporaneous evidence exists.
31Aga of Kish6256First Dynasty of Kish031The son of En-me-barage-si4Son of Enmebaragesi. Last ruler of the First Dynasty of Kish. The old Sumerian poem Gilgamesh and Aga descibes a Kishite siege of Uruk in response to Uruk's king, Gilgamesh, refusing to submit to Kish and provide its citizens as slaves to dig wells for Kish. The historicity of of this conflict is unknown.
